Robert Smith

     

     Robert Smith takes its title from the occasional-most-common name in the English-speaking world. It combines the advertising form of the “wacky inflatable tube man” with the seriousness of the aesthetic texture of a marble sculpture (though the object itself only simulates marble at its surface, where it is painted to appear as such). It looks down upon the viewer from behind a veil, staging a scene of judgment, shame, and amusement.
